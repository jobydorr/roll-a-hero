# House Style — how we write project documents

*Derived from Joby's own prose (the *Woven Kin* material) and from the rewrite of `CAMPAIGN.md` §1, which is the reference implementation. When in doubt, go read §1 and match it.*

*This governs written deliverables — campaign docs, world material, character and faction write-ups, briefs. It does not govern chat replies, which stay short and direct.*

---

## The target

**A formal book synopsis, a report, an essay.** Measured. Explanatory. Complete. The reader is an intelligent adult who wants to understand the thing, not be sold it.

Efficient does not mean clipped. **Economy comes from cutting redundancy, not from cutting connective tissue.**

## The rules

**Write in connected sentences.** Complete thoughts, joined with causal connectives — *because, and so, as a result, which means, in doing so*. Prose carries an argument from one idea to the next; the reader should never have to reassemble it.

**Do not use fragments for punch.** No "And it worked." No "They said no." No one-line paragraphs landing a beat. That rhythm is the single loudest tell of AI prose and it is not how Joby writes.

**State the fact, then say what it means.** This is the most characteristic move in his writing and it should appear regularly. *"The conflict between the guild and the Shad'ven serves as a microcosm of a larger struggle between tradition and progress. This tension creates an interesting subtext, allowing the story to comment on…"* Name the theme outright. Do not leave the significance implied and congratulate yourself for subtlety.

**Signpost structure before delivering it.** *"…and it rests on four locked conditions."* Then give the four. The reader is told the shape of the thing before being walked through it.

**Almost no bold.** Emphasis should be rare enough to mean something. If a paragraph has three bold phrases in it, the writing is doing the work with typography instead of sentences — rewrite the sentences.

**No interleaved DM asides.** No italic second-person coaching dropped into the middle of canon (*"Use this — it runs for two acts."*). If a note is for the DM, give it its own place. It does not belong wedged between two facts.

**Tiered summaries are welcome.** Joby's own documents use *"In brief:"* and *"Shortened version:"* freely. A long section followed by a three-sentence compression is good practice, not redundancy.

**Touchstones are a legitimate tool.** *"Yorian is a blend of Charles Xavier, Qui-Gon Jinn, and Gandalf."* Naming references is fast, precise, and useful at the table. Use it for NPCs and factions.

**American spellings.** *Center, defense, color.* (§1 sets this; the rest of the bible still has British forms to clean up.)

## Tone

**Dune meets Harry Potter, made like a Miyazaki film.** Measured and slightly elegiac, but never cold, and never grim for its own sake. The material can be sad and strange; it should not be bleak.

The document is written for an adult reader even though the game is for a table with a nine- and ten-year-old. **The prose does not need to be simple. The game does.** Do not confuse the two.

## The test

Read a paragraph aloud. If it sounds like advertising copy — short, punchy, escalating, every clause a hammer — it is wrong. If it sounds like a person patiently explaining something they have thought hard about, it is right.

---

## One structural note worth keeping

The *Woven Kin* material shows a recurring authorial preoccupation: **a founder whose creation escapes his control, an institution that chose power over truth, and a third path hiding in the margins.** The Foldwright is Yorian's tragedy carrying Ortheon's reputation.

This is not a criticism — it is the shape Joby's imagination keeps reaching for, and he already knows how to run it. But it is worth naming out loud, because our standing order is to refuse the generic and avoid recycling. **If a new villain, faction, or twist starts to fall into this same groove, notice it and say so.** Reuse should be a choice, not a default.
